Indian Wedding Planner Industry

 

Posted on 16th August, 2019

It doesn’t matter if we are hit by a pandemic like Covid-19 or facing some major recession, weddings will always continue to take place. That’s the reason which makes Weddings as a major business in India. The Indian Wedding Planning Industry includes many segments and their sub-segments such as Wedding Planning: Wedding Venues, Wedding Caterers, Wedding Decorations, Vendors, Wedding Themes, Wedding Matchmaking, Videography, Destination Wedding, etc..

Some major players of Indian Wedding Industry are:

 
  • 7Vachan
  • Bharat Matrimony
  • Elite Wedding Planners
  • Jeevansathi.com
  • Regal Weddings
  • Shaadi.com
  • ShaadiSaga
 

A recent KPMG report estimated India’s wedding market at $ 50 billion, with a rapid annual growth rate with around 10 million weddings organized every year. The future trends in the wedding planning industry would be Digital Weddings(Electronic Invitation cards, Personal Weddings website), Minimonies(Mini ceremonies with intimate family members), Open-air wedding venues, Pre-wedding shoots being more creative, health and safety concerns(Sanitized venues, PPE kits for makeup professionals, etc.)

Who would have thought of the Internet making perfect couples in a country like India? People here have always preferred to look out for referrals and contacts when it comes to fixing marriages but with evolution and more internet penetration, people do find it suitable to look out for online matchmaking platforms. In the era, where no one settles with less than what they think they deserve, they need plenty of options. With players like Matrimony.com, Jeevansathi.com, Shaadi.com, Simplymarry.com, it has become easier for the eligible bachelors to explore and choose their perfect partners online.

 

According to the KPMG report (2016), the marriage industry in India was estimated to be worth Rs. 3,681 Billion, which includes catering, decoration, venue, gifts and photography that accounts 61% of marriage expenses, whereas revenue in the Matchmaking segment is projected to reach US$184m in 2020 with a user base of 8.2M which makes India rank at the 4th position globally with China leading with rank 1 and a revenue of US$800m. Also, amidst this global pandemic of Covid-19 and lockdown, there was a sudden surge in sign-ups on online matchmaking platforms. According to the forecast made by Statista based on the impact of Covid-19, the revenue generated by Indian Matchmaking Industry is expected to reach US$269m in 2024 with a user base of 11.4M.

   

In India, at any given instant, around 60 million people are looking for their life partners, and about 10-12 million finally decide to get married every year in India. An average of 6-7 million of them register on online matchmaking websites and approximately 20% of them can find a perfect match as told by Mr. Murugavel, founder, Matrimony.com. He also said that the overall matchmaking sector in India is worth 1000 Crores. The leader in the Indian Matchmaking Industry is Matrimony.com at present having a market share of 60%, followed by Shaadi.com with a market share of 30% and Jeevansathi.com having a 10% of market share.
